Narrative:

This week our team had our usual weekly meeting and found no one had brought any snacks! Luckly, Outstanding Mouse saved the day and pointed us toward some SOM provided sandwiches outside our meeting, so the team did not work while hangry. Because of time constraints this week, including an impending final, this was a lower points week for us. Our team this week focused on setting up the Google Analytics and running the a/b testing this week. Our team started by creating a Google Analytics account under cautious-coyote’s credentials to generate the Google tracker. With this in hand, we added the Google generated code to our layout view file so that it would propagate across all pages of our site. A major malfunction occurred when cautious-coyote overwrote much of shy-wolverine’s work on the master branch of our published site. Having previously learned how to work off of old commits, our team had to do significant research to understand how to do forced pushes to the origin in order to update the head of the production code to a prior commit. With this resolved, the team was also able to add the “Donate Button” code and create a donation landing page.

This was also the first week we did not complete our sprint as planned. The team hoped to implement a/b testing this week but struggled to figure out how to implement it and was unable to make progress at office hours. We decided to wait until the video about a/b testing is posted. So, unfortunately this story is now part of our final sprint, but we consider the free food and drinks we got while visiting L400 to be the silver lining in our failed attempt to begin a/b testing.
